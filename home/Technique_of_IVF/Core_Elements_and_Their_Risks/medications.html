Gonadotropins

  • Purpose
    • Stimulate ovaries to induce growing eggs(oocytes) over 8+ days
  • Content
    • FSH = follicle stimulating hormone, stimulates follicle growth which contains eggs
    • LH = lutenizing horomone, works to increase progesterone
  • Risk
    • Risks associated with injection itself include bruising, redness, swelling, or discomfort
    • Bloating and discomfort as ovaries enlarge
    • Up to 2% develop OHSS (explained later in module – maybe provide a clickable link to explanation)
    • Fatigue, headache, weight gain, mood swings, nausea, clots in blood vessels
    • Risks of ovarian cancer associated with infertility itself and NOT necessarily w/ fertility meds (link to ovarian cx section here)

GnRH agonists

  • Purpose
    • The primary role of this medication is to prevent a premature LH surge, which could result in the release of eggs before they are ready to be retrieved
    • Can also be used to start the growth of the follicles or initiate the final stages of egg maturation
  • Content
    • Leuprolide acetate
  • Risk
    • Potential side effects experienced with long term use include, but are not limited to, hot flashes, vaginal dryness, bone loss, nausea, vomiting, skin reactions at injection site, fluid retention, muscle aches, headaches, and depression.
    • No long term or serious side effects are known
  • Administration
    • Injection (2 forms)
    • Short acting medication requiring daily injections
    • Long-acting preparation lasting 1-3 months
    • Discontinue use as soon as pregnancy is confirmed

GnRH antagonists

  • Purpose
    • To prevent premature ovulation
  • Content
    • Ganirelix Acetate or Cetrorelix Acetate
  • Risk
    • Potential side effects include, but are not limited to, abdominal pain, headaches, skin reaction at injection site, and nausea
  • Administration
    • Injection
    • Used for short periods of time in the late stages of ovarian stimulation

Human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) or Ovidrel

  • Purpose
    • Used in IVF cycles to induce the eggs to become mature and fertilizable
  • Content
    • hCG hormone
  • Risk
    • Potential side effects include, but are not limited to, breast tenderness, bloating, and pelvic discomfort
  • Administration
    • Injection
    • The timing of this medication is critical to retrieve mature eggs

Progesterone,estradiol

  • Purpose
    • Ensure adequate hormonal support of the uterine lining. The ovaries normally produce both hormones after ovulation. Supplemental progesterone is given after egg retrieval and continued for some weeks after pregnancy has been confirmed. In some cases supplemental estradiol is also given.
  • Content
    • Progesterone and estradiol
  • Risk
    • Progesterone
    • 1. Has NOT been associated with fetal abnormalities
    • Side effects include depression, sleepiness, allergic reaction, and if given by intramuscular injection possible infection or pain at site of application
    • Estradiol: nausea, irritation at injection site if given by trans-dermal route, risk of blood clots or stroke
  • Administration
    • Progesterone: injection or vaginal route after egg retrieval
    • Estradiol: oral, trans-dermal, intramuscular, or vaginal

Oral contraceptive pills/estradiol

  • Purpose
    • Many treatment protocols include oral contraceptive pills to be taken for 2 to 4 weeks or estradiol one to 4 weeks before gonadotropin injections are started in order to suppress hormone production or to schedule a cycle.
    • Estrace sometimes administered after ovulation prior to your menses, it is possible that it could be taken early in pregnancy. Safest course of action is to use a barrier method of contraception (condoms) the month you start estrace.
  • Content
    • No information given
  • Risk
    • Unscheduled bleeding, headache, breast tenderness, nausea, swelling, risk of blood clot and stroke
    • ii. Estrace has not been associated with any fetal malformations, but you my be told to discontinue use as soon as pregnancy is confirmed.
  • Administration
    • Oral

Other medications

    • Purpose
      • Given for short period of time to reduce risk of infection associated with egg retrieval or embryo transfer
    • Risk

  • Anti-anxiety or muscle relaxants
    • May be recommended prior to embryo transfer
    • Risk: most common side effect is drowsiness
